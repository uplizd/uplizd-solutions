## Quick Start
### 1) Import the Flow into Uplizd
1. Click the "Run on Uplizd" button above to open the solution template.
2. Select your preferred workspace and project destination.
3. Authenticate the required integrations within the Composio Toolset.
4. Ensure nodes are correctly mapped from the **Chat Input** through the **Agent** to the **Chat Output**.

### 2) Setup the Nodes
- **Chat Input**: Receives your specific research query or industry focus area.
- **Agent**: Processes the request, determines the necessary search parameters, and synthesizes the findings.
- **Composio Toolset**: Executes real-time web searches and data extraction from configured market intelligence sources.
- **Chat Output**: Delivers a structured, formatted report containing key trends and strategic recommendations.

### 3) Run the Flow
Use the Playground to test your analysis:
- `Analyze the current market trends for AI-driven CRM tools over the last 30 days.`
- `Compare the recent product updates from [Competitor A] and [Competitor B] and summarize the impact on our market share.`
- `Identify emerging risks in the SaaS sector based on the latest industry news and expert commentary.`

## Configuration
### 1) Language Model (Agent Node)
The agent acts as your dedicated market analyst.
- **Instruction Pattern**: Focus on objective synthesis rather than speculation.
- **Instruction Pattern**: Prioritize recent data points (last 7–30 days) to ensure relevance.
- **Instruction Pattern**: Structure output with clear headings, bulleted insights, and a "Strategic Takeaway" section.

### 2) Composio Toolset Node
- **API Key**: Ensure your Composio API key is active and has permissions for web search and news aggregation tools.
- **Connection Scope**: Enable read-only access to relevant market data APIs and search engines.

### 3) Tool Availability
- **Web Search**: For fetching the latest news and public announcements.
- **Data Summarizer**: For condensing long-form articles into actionable intelligence.
- **Trend Analyzer**: For identifying patterns across multiple data sources.
